# CAMELS streamflow rediscovery: Fish River near Fort Kent, ME

**Basin:** USGS 01013500 — Fish River near Fort Kent, ME
**Centroid:** lat=47.2375, lon=-68.5825
**Area:** 2253 km²
**Data:** 11,315 aligned daily samples (1990-01-01 → 2020-12-30)
**Target:** `log Q[t+1]` (next-day log streamflow)
**Forcing:** DAYMET single-pixel (precip, tmax, tmin)
**Streamflow:** USGS NWIS daily mean discharge
**Split:** chronological — TRAIN = first 8486 samples, TEST = remaining (2829)
**GP:** pop=300, gens=120, pointwise_only=False, runtime=4.6s

**TRAIN var(target) = 0.9554, TEST var(target) = 1.061**

## Baselines (TRAIN-fit, TRAIN+TEST evaluated)

| Baseline | TRAIN MSE | TRAIN %var | TEST MSE | TEST %var | TEST/TRAIN |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| predict mean | 0.9554 | 100.0% | 1.067 | 100.6% | 1.12 |
| persistence (Q[t+1]=Q[t]) | 0.007191 | 0.8% | 0.008276 | 0.8% | 1.15 |
| AR(1) with intercept | 0.007177 | 0.8% | 0.008262 | 0.8% | 1.15 |
| multi-lag linear (P lags 0..7 + Q) | 0.004691 | 0.5% | 0.00553 | 0.5% | 1.18 |

Multi-lag linear is the *engineering* baseline — what a
hydrologist would build in 10 lines of `numpy.linalg.lstsq`.
The GP needs to beat this AND generalize to TEST.

**Reported LSTM benchmark (citation, not run): Kratzert et al.
(2019) EA-LSTM on 531-basin CAMELS subset, median NSE ≈ 0.74**
on TEST period 1989-1999. NSE = 1 - MSE/var ≈ a TEST %var of ~26%.
Tessera's discrete-tree SR is not expected to match an LSTM's
expressive ceiling, but the *form* it discovers is interpretable
where the LSTM's parameters aren't.

## Reading the result

- The engineering baseline (multi-lag linear) is what a
  hydrologist would write — already captures the convolution
  form at a fixed lag basis. GP needs to do BETTER than this
  to claim mechanism discovery; doing better than persistence
  alone is the trivial bar.
- A Class-C result means: discovered a convolution-shaped
  expression AND it generalizes to TEST years. The shape of
  the discovered Measure (EMA half-life, lag) is then
  interpretable as the basin's effective IUH timescale.
- A Class-B result would use trajectory-specific scalars
  (reduce_max(Q_train) etc.) that don't transfer to TEST.
  Same failure mode the `reduce_*` downweight (CHANGELOG
  2026-05-25) was designed to suppress.
